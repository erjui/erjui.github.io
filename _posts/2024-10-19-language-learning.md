# Language Learning Methods

I've been interested in learning new languages for a long time. Since I recently started my personal blog here, I thought it would be a good idea to write about my recent attempts on my language learning journey 😎.

## 1. How to Scale Up Time Spent on Language Learning

I've tried a few language learning methods so far. In this post, I want to share one of the methods that I've recently come up with: how to scale up time spent on language learning.

As a language learner who lives in a country where English is not the native language, it's not easy to find time to learn a new language. Especially when you work for a living, it's hard to find time to learn a new language. Thus, I've been planning to do things on my to-do list and intentionally spend time on language learning. However, I found that it's not very effective. For example, even though I study 10 minutes on language learning every day, such as listening to podcasts or watching videos, I don't see significant improvement in my language skills. I got frustrated and envious of people who are living in a country where English is the native language and they can speak English fluently.

> **Note:** Before I elaborate on my idea, I want to make clear that I'm not a language learning expert. I'm just a language learner who is trying to find a way to learn a new language more effectively.

### The Interesting Idea

What I found interesting is that native English speakers don't spend much time on language learning; they just 🎵 listen to English songs, 🎬 watch English movies and TV shows, and 💬 talk to each other in English.

And they are able to speak English fluently without even realizing it. They don't have any special language learning methods. They just do what they enjoy and keep doing it day by day, spending more than 8 hours immersively.

So I thought that if I want to speak English fluently, I should do the same thing. I should listen to English songs, watch English movies and TV shows, and talk to myself in English. And I should keep doing it day by day, spending more than 8 hours immersively. However, it's not so easy to do that because I have my own job, and it's very hard to find a lot of time to do that. Thus, I came up with the following assumptions and ideas.

### Assumptions

1. I've been spending **10 minutes** on language learning every day. If I can scale up the time spent on language learning to **8 hours** a day, it is literally **48 times** more effective.
2. People think inside their own head with their own language. That is, even when I speak English, it's highly likely that I think in Korean.
3. Just thinking and creating sentences in my head in the target language would be effective when it comes to improving my language skills.

### Idea

Throughout all of my daily activities, I will try to think in English. The basic idea is that by consciously shifting my inner monologue to English, I can dramatically increase my exposure to and practice with the language. This includes:

- Narrating and describing things that I see, hear, and experience in English.
- Formulating my thoughts and plans in English.
- Translating any native language thoughts into English immediately.
  - When having a conversation with my friends or colleagues, it would be hard to always think in English because I have to respond immediately. However, when I'm not in a conversation, I can try to translate what other people say in my head into English.

## 2. Shadowing Experiment

Since this month, I decided to try shadowing. Shadowing is a technique where you listen to the audio and repeat after the speaker instantly. It's a well-known technique in the language learning community. I decided to try shadowing because I thought it might be a good way to improve my pronunciation and accent.

The purpose of shadowing is to improve your pronunciation by following the speaker's intonation and rhythm. In detail, I decided to choose one of the YouTubers whom I like to follow and try to shadow their videos. Because I want to have a North American accent, I chose a YouTuber who is from North America (the YouTuber is from Canada, by the way, and I don't think I can share the exact channel here 🤔).

I don't know whether it will work for me or not, but I think it would be a good idea to keep trying and see how it goes. So I will keep you all posted about my progress on this experiment in my future posts.
