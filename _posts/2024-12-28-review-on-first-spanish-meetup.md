## Introduction

Last Sunday, I attended my first Spanish language exchange meetup. It was my first time meeting people who speak Spanish, and also my first time to speak in front of people in Spanish. Because I think it's a good opportunity to review my learning progress, I decided to write a post about my experience, what I learned, possible improvements in the future. In this post, I will share my thoughts based on the few keywords that I felt most important lessons they were. Vamos a ver!

## Few things I felt important

### Listening is the most important skill when it comes to communication

One of the amazing thing when I went to the meetup was that I could listen almost 80-90% of what people were saying. Becuase it was literally my first time for real person to talk to me in Spanish, I was so excited and satisfied with my progress. Come to think of it, I think listening a lot of Spanish podcasts and videos really helped me to understand the language. Even though I was having few troubles with words, I could understand the meaning of the sentence basically.

So the first thing I want to mention here is that, listening is the most important and powerful skill when it comes to talk with people in Spanish. I am not saying that speaking is not important when it comes to communicating, but if you can't listen to what people are saying, you can't respond to them in the first place. I felt really safe and comfortable myself at the meetup, because I could understand what people were saying and I could respond and practice my speaking skills becuase I could listen to what they were saying. That's the first thing I want to emphasize and the thing I believe I did right.

### Practice should include, but not limited to speaking about myself

Before going to my first meetup, I was so nervous becuase I had no experience speaking Spanish, that I only practiced listening to a lot of Spanish podcasts and videos. Thus, to anticipate the meetup, I practiced making sentences on my own. What I did mostly was to prepare possible questions and phrases that can appear in the meetup, and prepared my own answers and thoughts in Spanish.

It worked, unless I was only able to speak what I prepared. That being said, I practiced to talk about myself, my name, my background, my hobbies, and my goals. Also I also just practiced to answer the questions that I prepared. There comes a problem, that I was not even able to question the other person about very simple and basic things. Even though I felt safe and comfortable at the meetup, It was very shame that I couldn't ask the other person about simple stuff.

At the end of the day, I want to say that I should have practiced more making sentences on a variety of forms such as first person plural, second and third person, also questioning as well.

### Communication surprisingly does not require a lot of skills

I am not good at *speaking* in Spanish. I am not good at *listening* neither. I am still having a lot of troubles whenever I watch videos in Youtube. But surprisingly, I could communicate with people at the meetup. This is not only because I could listen to most of what people were saying, but also the requirement of communication is not as high as people think.

People think that communication and speaking with native speakers is a very hard thing to do, which requires a high level of target language skills. But as I have been thinking as well as as I experienced in the meetup, I could communicate, make good conversions, and even was able to make a new friend. What I want to say here is that, generally (most of the) communication does not require a lot of skills, whereas I just need to know frequently used words and phrases with a proper set of speaking and listening skills. Therefore, I think one can start practice real-time communication with native speakers sooner than people might think. And this was where it begins for me to decide to join the meetup. And it worked!

## Conclusion

From this experience, I felt that I should have practiced more making sentences on a variety of forms. I firmly believe that is where the learning progress is made. By deciding to join the meetup, I had no choice but to practice a lot not to feel embarrassed, and I could make a lot of progress. And by talking with real people who speak Spanish, I could feel with my own skin that I am progressing in the right direction, as well as there are some improvements that I need to make which I will practice for the next meetup.

All in all, It was genuinely one of the most fun and interesting experiences I have ever had. I learned a lot of things, and I could make a new friend there. And also I am looking forward to the next meetup, and I will practice more to make more progress.

*La práctica hace al maestro. Hasta la próxima!*
