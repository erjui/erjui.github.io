# Introduction

After traveling to several African countries following my graduation, I developed a strong interest in learning new languages. Meeting people from different cultures and languages during my travels sparked my curiosity and passion for language learning. Because I was already spending a lot of hours learning English, I decided to focus on Spanish, the second most spoken language in the world. And this is how my journey with Spanish started. So far, I have spent about 6 months learning Spanish. Along the way, I have tried different resources and techniques to learn Spanish, and I realized that there are efficient ways to learn a language in each stage of learning journey. Thus, in this post, I want to share my experiences and progress during my first 6 months of learning Spanish.

The purpose of this post is not to give you a step by step guide on how to learn Spanish, but rather to share my personal experiences and reflections on my journey with Spanish. So that I can track my progress and learn from my mistakes, and improve my learning techniques. Let's dive into my journey with Spanish! 🛫

## My Learning Journey

### First Period (Months 1-2)

My Spanish learning journey began in May 2024, when I decided to dedicate myself to mastering this beautiful language. For starters, I started with Duolingo, a language learning app famous for its approach as a game. In this stage, I know literally nothing about Spanish, so I focused on building my vocabulary and understanding the basics of the language.

Firstly I want to stress out that Duolingo is a great resource for beginners, as it provides a structured approach to learning a language. However, along the way, I realized that this app has some drawbacks for person like me who is trying to learn a language faster than the app provides. For example, the app keeps giving me the same exercises over and over again, where I have to repeat the same thing for several times. Also, the context of the exercises is not appropriate for daily conversations, which is the main purpose of learning a language.

Obviously, Duolingo is not a bad app, and it helped me a lot in the beginning of my learning journey. And I decided to move on to other strategies to learn Spanish after 2 months of using Duolingo.

### Second Period (Months 3)

As my skills improved, I decided to focus intensively on listening skills. Because my main goal is to communicate with native speakers, I think listening is the most important skill to improve. I definitely think reading is also an important skill, but I think writing requires a lot of time and effort, with a lot of vocabulary which is not used day to day. Writing and speaking are also things that I want to improve, but I think I need to build a strong foundation first to start them. Grammar, on the other hand, was not a concern for me at this stage, because I want to focus on my main goal, which is to understand and be understood by native speakers, not a perfect grammar knowledge.

Now that I decided to focus on listening skills, I started to find resources to improve my listening skills. First thing I tried to find Spanish youtube channels, and I watched them with Spanish subtitles. Out of several channels, I found "Eash Spanish" channel helpful for beginners. I watched videos about daily activities, and I tried to understand what they say by reading the subtitles.

In summary, I think I tried to find what suits me the best. And it follows from the next period that I am in now.

### Current Period (Months 4-6)

After trying several resources, I found that listening to Spanish podcasts is the great way for me to improve my listening skills. And it follows from the next period that I am still using podcasts to improve my listening skills. In this period, I listened to podcasts with Spanish and English subtitles, which allows me to understand the content and improve my listening skills. And I started with listening to one podcast a day, gradually increasing the number of podcasts I listen to as my skills improve. Usually, each podcast is about 20~30 minutes, and I listened to them during my daily commute.

While podcasts are one of the best resources for me to improve my listening skills, It requires a lot of time to listen to them. At first, just listening to one podcast took more than 1 hour, and I was not able to understand most of the content. I had to search all the words I don't know, wrote down the new words and expressions, and review them later, which made me take more time to review them. So I gradually came up with a strategy to learn as much as possible with the limited time I have.

First approach I took when I first started listening to podcasts is to write down all the new words and expressions as I mentioned before. As I mentioned, this strategy requires a lot of time, and even I was not able to finish one podcast in a single day. So I had to find a way to reduce the time I spent on this. The next thing I tried is to not write down all the new words and expressions, rather just focus on the main point of the podcast, comprehend the whole context of the podcast. With this strategy, I can be exposed to more content in a given amount of time, where I encounter more frequently used words and expressions natually without writing them down. After I got used to it, I started to listen to more podcasts a day, cause the frequency of stopping to search for the words I don't know is getting less and less. Then the next problem appeared, which is that I'm kinda not having enough time to listen to the multiple podcasts everyday. So from here, I decided to listen to podcasts with faster speed, starting from 1.2x and gradually increasing to 1.5x speed. At the time I decided to do this, I was able to understand quite a lot of content, where there are few times I have to stop to search for words I don't know. So the strategy basically is to focus on harder vocabulary and expressions with comprehension of the context, while increasing the playback speed to listen the easier parts of the podcast.

One other minor thing is that I started to practice my writing skills at the same time. The way I tried is to write journals whenever I have some idea or thoughts. And this is one of the strategies that I found on the second period. And as you see, I already posted some short and simple journals in this blog as well 😗.

### Current concerns

Now that I am being comfortable with the strategies I came up with, I realaized that the time is coming to move on to the next stage of my learning journey, which is to improve my speaking skills. Actually, on the second period, I found several methods to practice my speaking skills as well, such as going to offline meetups (I already found one, but I haven't been able to go yet) or use AI tools to practice my speaking skills.

Another concern is to create a more structured sentences for writing and speaking. After writing few journals, I found that my sentences are not very grammaticallly correct and structured. So I think I need to work on this, and try to find or come up with (my own) strategies to improve my writing and speaking skills.


# Conclusion

Looking back on my first 6 months of learning Spanish, I can say that it has been an exciting and rewarding journey. Through trial and error, I've discovered learning methods that work best for me - starting with basic apps like Duolingo, transitioning to focused listening practice with YouTube videos, and finally finding resources like Spanish podcasts. While I still have a long way to go, I'm proud of the progress I've made so far, and I'm looking forward to the next stage of my learning journey.

I'm excited to continue this journey and take on new challenges in my Spanish learning adventure. Stay tuned for my next post! ¡Hasta la próxima! 👋
